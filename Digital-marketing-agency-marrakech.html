The Business Case

Why Marrakech Tourism Businesses
Need Digital Marketing in 2026

Marrakech welcomed over 4.2 million international tourists in 2025, with projections for continued growth in 2026. Nearly every one of those travelers found their accommodation, restaurant, or activity through a single channel: Google.

Yet the vast majority of riads, restaurants, spas, and tour operators in Marrakech either have no digital presence, rely entirely on Booking.com and TripAdvisor, or have a website that hasn't been updated since 2019. This is not just a missed opportunity — it's a structural disadvantage that costs real money every single day.

The Commission Math

A riad charging 1,200 MAD/night at 80% occupancy generates ~230,400 MAD/month gross. At 20% average OTA commission, that's 46,080 MAD paid to platforms every month — over half a million MAD per year. A comprehensive digital marketing strategy costs a fraction of that, permanently.

The Marrakech Digital Gap

Marrakech's tourism ecosystem is unusually under-digitized compared to European or Southeast Asian equivalents. Most riads appear on Google Maps with incomplete profiles and no website. Most restaurants rely exclusively on TripAdvisor reviews. Most spas and hammams have zero organic search presence. This creates an exceptional opportunity: businesses that invest in digital marketing now can own category-defining positions in Marrakech's Google results for years, well ahead of competitors who remain OTA-dependent.

Process

How ProBoost Grows
Your Digital Presence

  1. Free Audit — Your Starting Point

    We analyze your current Google rankings, website performance (speed, mobile, SEO structure), Google Business Profile completeness, competitor positions, and which keywords represent the biggest opportunities for your specific business in Marrakech. You receive a written summary — no jargon, no sales pressure.

  2. Strategy & Keyword Map

    Based on the audit, we build a custom keyword map targeting the searches your potential guests are actually making — in English, French, and Arabic. We prioritize by search volume, competition level, and commercial intent, then plan the content and technical work needed to capture those positions.

  3. Technical Foundation

    We fix what's broken before building what's new. This typically includes: page speed optimization, mobile responsiveness, HTTPS, structured data (schema markup), XML sitemap submission to Google Search Console, and Google Business Profile cleanup. This work creates the foundation that makes everything else more effective.

  4. Content & On-Page Optimization

    Every page of your website is rewritten with SEO structure: keyword-optimized titles, meta descriptions, header hierarchy, image alt texts, internal links, and page-level keyword focus. New pages and blog posts are created targeting high-value keywords identified in the audit. This is where most rankings are won.

  5. Authority Building

    We build backlinks through outreach to relevant travel media, bloggers, and Moroccan tourism directories. We also build local citations — consistent NAP listings across TripAdvisor, Yelp, Foursquare, and Morocco-specific directories — that strengthen your Google Maps ranking.

  6. Monthly Reporting & Iteration

    Each month you receive a clear report: keyword ranking movements, organic traffic growth, direct bookings attributed to organic search, and the next month's priorities. SEO is iterative — what we learn from month 1 makes month 2 more effective, compounding over time.
