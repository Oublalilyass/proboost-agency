Understanding Marrakech
Airbnb Performance Data

Why Benchmark Your Marrakech Airbnb?

Most Airbnb hosts in Marrakech operate with no visibility into how their listing actually performs versus the local market. They see their own bookings calendar, but have no reference point — is 50% occupancy good or bad for a 2-bedroom apartment in Guéliz? Is 1,400 MAD/night competitive for a riad in the Medina?

This benchmark tool solves that blind spot. By comparing your specific neighborhood and bedroom count against aggregated market data, you get an instant, objective read on whether you're leaving revenue on the table or already outperforming your peers.

Marrakech listings average around 1,260 MAD per night, with top performers achieving 62–67% occupancy versus the 52% market average.

What Drives the Occupancy Gap?

The gap between average performers (52% occupancy) and top performers (62–67% occupancy) in Marrakech is rarely about luck. It typically comes down to five controllable factors:

  • Photography quality: Listings with professional photography see 20–40% higher booking conversion than those with phone photos.
  • Pricing strategy: Dynamic, demand-responsive pricing (adjusting for weekends, holidays, events) consistently outperforms flat-rate pricing by 15–25% in annual revenue.
  • Response time & Superhost status: Hosts who respond within 1 hour and maintain Superhost status see meaningfully higher search ranking within Airbnb's algorithm.
  • Review velocity: Listings accumulating 5-star reviews quickly in the first 90 days build algorithmic trust faster, compounding visibility over time.
  • Direct booking presence: Properties with their own booking website capture repeat guests and reduce dependency on OTA commission (15-25% per booking).

Neighborhood-by-Neighborhood Breakdown

Performance varies dramatically across Marrakech. The Medina commands the highest average daily rates thanks to proximity to Jemaa el-Fnaa and authentic riad inventory, but also faces the most direct competition from thousands of similar listings. Hivernage benefits from upscale positioning and proximity to nightlife and dining, posting strong occupancy with mid-to-high ADR.

Guéliz — the modern city center — offers the most balanced risk/reward profile: solid occupancy, moderate ADR, and strong demand from business travelers and digital nomads year-round. Palmeraie and Agdal skew toward luxury villas with pools, commanding premium rates but lower occupancy due to a smaller addressable guest pool (families, larger groups).

How to Use This Data to Improve Your Listing

If your benchmark comparison shows you're underperforming the market average, here's a prioritized action plan:

  • If your occupancy is low but ADR is competitive: The problem is visibility or conversion — invest in photography, optimize your title/description, and review your response time metrics.
  • If your ADR is below market but occupancy is high: You're likely underpricing. Gradually test rate increases of 5-10% while monitoring booking velocity.
  • If both are below market: Consider a full listing audit — professional photos, pricing tool implementation (PriceLabs, Beyond Pricing), and a review acceleration strategy.
  • If you're already at or above market average: Focus on revenue management — explore direct booking channels to reduce OTA commission and protect your margin at scale.
