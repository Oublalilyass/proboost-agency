Why Direct Bookings Are Worth Fighting For

Pourquoi les Réservations Directes Valent la Peine de se Battre

The Hidden Cost of OTA Commissions in Morocco

Every time a guest books your riad, hotel, or tour through Booking.com, Airbnb, or Expedia, a significant portion of your revenue permanently transfers to a platform based thousands of miles from Marrakech. What many hospitality owners don't realize is just how significant this cost is cumulatively.

Booking.com charges 15% commission on total booking value. Expedia operates at 15–18%. GetYourGuide charges tour operators 25–30%. On a €300 booking, that's €45–€90 per booking — every booking, 365 days a year. For a medium-sized riad or hotel processing 50 bookings per month at €280 average, the annual OTA commission bill exceeds €25,200. That figure alone could fund a complete SEO campaign with substantial ROI remaining.

How Direct Bookings Transform Your Profitability

The math is simple but powerful. If your average booking nets €200 after OTA commission, and a direct booking nets €240 for the exact same stay, you need 20% fewer direct bookings to generate the same revenue. Or differently: lower your direct price by 10%, still earn more per booking than through OTAs, and convert more price-sensitive travelers to loyal returning guests.

  • OTAs charge 15–25% commission with zero reduction for your operational costs
  • Price parity clauses on some platforms prevent competitive direct pricing
  • You don't own the guest relationship — OTAs control the email and booking data
  • A single algorithm change can reduce your visibility with no recourse
  • Review systems are controlled by platforms, not your brand

The Realistic Path to 40–60% Direct Bookings

The goal isn't to eliminate OTAs — they have genuine reach advantages for new guest acquisition. The goal is to rebalance the mix so that 40–60% of bookings come direct, dramatically improving profitability while maintaining the volume OTAs provide.

Properties achieving a healthy direct booking ratio report: better cash flow, stronger guest relationships, higher lifetime guest value, and significantly lower effective customer acquisition costs. For a property currently generating €200,000 annually through OTAs at 15% commission, shifting 40% to direct saves approximately €12,000 per year in commissions alone — without a single additional booking.
