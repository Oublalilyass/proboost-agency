The Foundation

Why Local SEO Is the
Highest-ROI Investment for Marrakech Tourism

When a traveler in Paris, London, or Dubai types "riad Marrakech Medina" into Google, three things happen. Google evaluates every riad website and business profile for local relevance. It selects three to show in the Local Pack — the map block that appears above all organic results. And 44% of all clicks on that search page go to those three results.

The business in position one of the Local Pack for "riad Marrakech" receives a volume of free, high-intent traffic that would cost thousands of euros in Google Ads to replicate. Local SEO is the mechanism that puts you in that position — and keeps you there.

The Marrakech Local SEO opportunity

Marrakech's tourism market generates enormous local search volume: "restaurant Medina Marrakech" (6,200/mo), "spa Marrakech" (5,400/mo), "riad Marrakech" (22,000/mo). Yet the vast majority of local businesses have incomplete Google Business Profiles, inconsistent NAP data, and no local SEO strategy whatsoever. The gap between what travelers search for and what Marrakech businesses offer digitally is the Local SEO opportunity.

Unlike paid advertising, local SEO compounds. A strong local ranking built over 6 months does not disappear when you stop paying — it becomes a permanent asset. Every review, every citation, every piece of local content adds to a foundation that grows more valuable and more difficult for competitors to displace over time.

Understanding the Algorithm

How Google Decides Who
Ranks #1 in Marrakech

Google's Local Pack algorithm weighs three core factors, known as the Relevance, Distance, and Prominence framework. Understanding how each factor applies in Marrakech is the foundation of every decision in this checklist.

FactorWhat Google MeasuresHow to Win It in MarrakechWeight
Relevance How well your business matches the search intent — category, keywords, services described Primary GBP category precision, keyword-rich description, service attributes, menu/rooms listed High
Distance How close your business is to the searcher's location or the location mentioned in the query Accurate address, neighborhood keywords (Medina, Mouassine, Gueliz), geo-coordinates in schema Medium
Prominence How well-known and trusted your business is — reviews, backlinks, citations, online mentions Review volume + velocity, local citations, travel media mentions, domain authority High
Behavioral signals Click-through rate, direction requests, phone calls, photo views from your GBP listing Compelling GBP photos, strong review average, complete information that earns clicks Growing
The Marrakech-specific factor

In Marrakech's Medina, many businesses share very similar addresses — derbs (alleys) without precise street numbering create NAP ambiguity that confuses Google's distance algorithm. Providing precise GPS coordinates in your schema markup is more important in Marrakech than in any other Moroccan city, and is a differentiator that most competitors miss entirely.

Google Business Profile — The Foundation
12 items · Highest single impact on Local Pack ranking
Critical
Claim and verify your Google Business Profile
Go to business.google.com and claim your listing. Verification by postcard takes 5–14 days in Marrakech — request it immediately if not yet done. Unverified listings do not rank in the Local Pack.
Free
Select the most precise primary business category
For riads: "Bed and breakfast inn" or "Boutique hotel". For restaurants: "Moroccan restaurant" as primary. For spas: "Day spa". The primary category is Google's strongest relevance signal — be as specific as your actual service, not what sounds most impressive.
Free
Add all relevant secondary categories
A riad can be both "Boutique hotel" AND "Bed and breakfast inn" AND "Vacation rental". A restaurant with a rooftop bar can add "Bar" as secondary. Secondary categories expand your keyword surface area without diluting your primary relevance signal.
Free
Write a keyword-rich business description (750 characters)
Include your primary keyword naturally in the first sentence: "Riad Al Andalus is a boutique riad in the heart of Marrakech's Medina..." Then describe your key differentiators, neighborhood, and amenities. Include French phrases — Google indexes the description for multilingual search.
Free
Add 40+ high-quality photos across all categories
GBP profiles with 100+ photos receive 520% more direction requests and 2,717% more phone calls than those with no photos. Upload: exterior, interior, rooms/tables, courtyard, rooftop, food, staff, neighbourhood. Name files descriptively before uploading (riad-marrakech-courtyard.jpg).
Free
Set precise opening hours including special hours for Ramadan
Inaccurate hours are the #1 reason for negative "closed when it should be open" reviews — which tank your ranking. Set standard hours, update for Ramadan (post-iftar service), and add public holiday hours for Eid. Enable "Open 24 hours" for riads if reception is staffed overnight.
Free
Enable GBP messaging and respond within 1 hour
Google measures response time as a quality signal. Enable messaging, set up an automated response that acknowledges the inquiry and sets expectations ("We'll reply within 2 hours"), and ensure someone monitors messages. Accounts with fast response time receive preferential placement.
Free
Add all services, amenities and attributes
GBP provides attribute checklists for your category. For riads: pool, WiFi, parking, breakfast included, air conditioning, rooftop terrace. For restaurants: outdoor seating, reservation required, alcohol served, vegetarian options. Each attribute adds to your keyword surface area for relevant searches.
Free
Add your menu (restaurants) or room types (riads)
GBP allows direct menu upload for restaurants and product/service listings for accommodation. A complete menu with dish names and prices significantly increases "menu" and dish-name search visibility. For riads, list each room type with a brief description and price range.
Free
Publish 1 Google Post per week minimum
Google Posts appear in your GBP panel in search results. Weekly posts on offers, seasonal availability, events, or property features signal an active, managed profile — which Google rewards with preferential ranking. Include a CTA button (Book, Call, Learn More) and a high-quality photo on every post.
Free
Populate the Q&A section proactively
Anyone can answer Q&A on your listing — including competitors. Ask and answer the 8–10 most common guest questions yourself first: check-in time, parking, pool access, whether alcohol is served, distance from Jemaa el-Fnaa. Keyword-rich Q&A answers are indexed by Google.
Free
Check for and merge duplicate GBP listings
Duplicate listings are extremely common in Marrakech's Medina — old submissions, different transliterations of Arabic names, or former owners' listings still active. Duplicates split your review count and ranking authority. Search your exact business name + Marrakech on Google Maps and flag any duplicate for removal via Google Business support.
Free
2
NAP Consistency — Name, Address, Phone
6 items · Foundation of local trust signals
Critical
Define your canonical NAP format in writing
Decide on one exact format: your business name (no abbreviations, no punctuation variations), your address (in both French and English transliteration if applicable), and your phone number in international format (+212...). Write it down. Every future mention of your business anywhere online must match exactly.
Free
Audit NAP consistency across all major platforms
Check and correct: Google Business Profile, your website footer, Booking.com, Airbnb, TripAdvisor, Yelp, Facebook Business Page, and any directory listings. Even minor differences ("+212 6" vs "+2126" vs "06...") dilute local authority. Update every discrepancy to match your canonical format.
Free
Add your precise GPS coordinates to your website schema
Critical for Marrakech's Medina where derb addresses are imprecise. Get your exact coordinates from Google Maps (right-click your location → "What's here?") and add them to your LocalBusiness schema as GeoCoordinates. This gives Google a definitive location signal that overrides address ambiguity.
Technical
Add NAP to your website footer on every page
Your exact business name, address, and phone number should appear in the footer of every page of your website — not just the contact page. Use consistent formatting. Google crawls footer content on every page and uses it to verify the NAP data in your GBP listing.
Technical
Include your neighborhood in your address descriptions
For Marrakech Medina addresses, include the quarter name: "Mouassine Quarter, Marrakech Medina" or "Near Jemaa el-Fnaa, Marrakech". Neighborhood terms appear in many local search queries and including them in your address context improves relevance for those searches.
Free
Set up WhatsApp Business with your canonical phone number
WhatsApp Business is Morocco's primary communication channel — and your WhatsApp Business phone number must match your canonical NAP exactly. Set up your business profile, catalog, and automated messages. This is both a NAP consistency action and a direct booking conversion tool.
Free

Review Strategy — The Ranking Fuel
6 items · Second-highest impact factor after GBP completeness
Critical
Build a systematic post-checkout review request process
Reviews don't happen by accident — they require a repeatable system. The optimal Marrakech process: ask verbally at checkout, send a WhatsApp follow-up 48 hours later with a direct Google review link (g.page/[yourbusiness]/review), and include a QR code card in rooms/on tables linking directly to your review form. Consistency over intensity.
Free
Target 100+ Google reviews with 4.7+ average rating
In Marrakech's Local Pack competition, 100+ Google reviews at 4.7+ stars is the threshold above which ranking position becomes consistently dominant. Businesses below 50 reviews struggle to hold Local Pack position even with excellent GBP profiles. Review velocity (getting new reviews consistently over time) matters as much as total count.
Free
Respond to 100% of reviews within 24 hours
Google measures owner response rate as a prominence signal. Respond to every review — positive and negative — within 24 hours. For positive reviews: thank, personalize (mention their name, something specific about their stay), and invite return. For negative reviews: apologize, take responsibility, offer a resolution offline. Never argue publicly.
Free
Include keywords naturally in review response text
Your review response text is indexed by Google. When responding, naturally include your target keywords: "Thank you for staying at our riad in Marrakech's Medina..." or "We're glad our traditional Moroccan hammam experience exceeded expectations." This adds keyword-rich content to your GBP that compounds over hundreds of responses.
Free
Generate reviews in French, English AND Arabic
Multilingual reviews signal to Google that your business serves an international audience — which is relevant for Marrakech's international tourism search queries. Actively encourage French-speaking guests to leave reviews in French (Google shows reviews in the searcher's language), and Arabic-language reviews strengthen your visibility for Gulf market searches.
Free
Monitor and manage TripAdvisor reviews independently
TripAdvisor review ranking directly impacts Google search results for "[your business name] reviews" queries, and TripAdvisor Travellers' Choice badges appear in GBP listings, significantly improving CTR. Treat TripAdvisor reviews with the same urgency as Google reviews — same response speed, same quality of engagement.
Free

On-Page Local SEO Signals
7 items · Your website as a local ranking asset
High Impact
Include city + neighborhood in every title tag
Every page title should include location context. Homepage: "[Riad Name] — Boutique Riad in Marrakech Medina | Official Site". Room page: "Dar Suite with Terrace — Riad in Mouassine, Marrakech". The location signal in the title tag is one of the strongest on-page local SEO factors and is missing from most Marrakech tourism websites.
Technical
Write a dedicated "Location" page for your website
Create a standalone page titled "Our Location in Marrakech Medina" with: your full address, an embedded Google Map, written directions from the airport and from Jemaa el-Fnaa, your neighborhood description, nearby landmarks, and parking notes. This page targets "where is [riad name]" queries and provides strong location context to Google's crawler.
Content
Use local keywords naturally in body content
Your homepage body text should reference Marrakech, the Medina, your specific neighbourhood, and nearby landmarks naturally — not forced. "Our riad is located 3 minutes' walk from Jemaa el-Fnaa in the Mouassine quarter of Marrakech's historic Medina" provides multiple local signals in a single sentence without keyword stuffing.
Content
Embed a Google Map on your contact/location page
An embedded Google Map on your website creates a direct association between your website domain and your GBP location in Google's data model. Use your GBP map embed (not a generic Marrakech map). This is a simple technical action with a measurable impact on local ranking consistency.
Technical
Create French-language versions of core pages
French-language content targeting "riad Marrakech Medina", "restaurant rooftop Marrakech" and similar FR queries is essential for the largest Marrakech tourism market. Implement hreflang tags (<link rel="alternate" hreflang="fr">) to tell Google which language version to show to French-speaking searchers. This is local SEO AND international SEO simultaneously.
Technical
Write local content blog posts (neighborhood guides, local tips)
Blog posts targeting local informational keywords — "Best things to do near [your neighbourhood] in Marrakech", "How to find the best riads in the Medina", "Marrakech souks: a beginner's guide" — build topical authority around Marrakech that strengthens all your local rankings simultaneously. Two quality posts per month is the minimum effective cadence.
Content
Optimize all images with location-specific alt text and filenames
Every image on your website should have a descriptive filename including location: "riad-marrakech-medina-courtyard-zellige.jpg" and alt text like "Zellige-tiled courtyard of our riad in Marrakech's Mouassine quarter". Google Images is a significant local discovery channel — particularly for visual businesses like riads and restaurants.
Technical

Schema Markup — Structured Data
4 items · Tell Google exactly what your business is and where
High Impact
Implement LocalBusiness schema on your homepage
Add JSON-LD LocalBusiness schema to your homepage <head> with: name, url, telephone, address (PostalAddress), geo (GeoCoordinates with your precise lat/long), openingHours, priceRange, and servesCuisine (restaurants) or amenityFeature (riads). This is the single most technically impactful local SEO action available.
Technical
Add FAQPage schema to your FAQ content
FAQ schema enables rich snippets — expanded search results that display your Q&A directly in Google's results page, dramatically increasing click-through rate. Target the questions travelers actually search: "How far is [your riad] from Jemaa el-Fnaa?", "Does [your restaurant] take reservations?", "Is breakfast included?"
Technical
Add geo meta tags to every page
Add these meta tags to your HTML head on every page: <meta name="geo.region" content="MA-MAR">, <meta name="geo.placename" content="Marrakech, Morocco">, <meta name="geo.position" content="31.6295;-7.9811">, and <meta name="ICBM" content="31.6295, -7.9811">. These are parsed by Google's local search crawler to confirm your geographic context.
Technical
Add Review / AggregateRating schema if you have testimonials
If you display guest reviews or ratings on your website, implement AggregateRating schema showing your average rating and review count. Google may display star ratings directly in search results (rich snippets), which can increase CTR by 20–30% for local search results.
