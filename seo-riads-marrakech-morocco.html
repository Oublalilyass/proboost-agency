On-Page Optimization

Optimizing Your Riad Website
for Google's Ranking Signals

On-page SEO for riads is about giving Google every possible signal that your website is the most relevant, trustworthy, and authoritative result for someone searching for a riad in Marrakech. Here's the complete framework:

  1. Title Tags and Meta Descriptions

    Every page needs a unique, keyword-rich title tag under 60 characters and a compelling meta description under 155 characters. Your homepage title should follow the pattern: [Riad Name] — Boutique Riad in Marrakech Medina | Official Site. Include "official site" or "book direct" to signal direct booking intent and reduce Booking.com click-throughs.

  2. H1 and Header Structure

    Each page gets one H1 containing your primary keyword. For the homepage, this is typically "Authentic Riad in the Heart of Marrakech's Medina" — not your riad's name. Use H2s for room types and amenities, H3s for specifics. Headers create a scannable structure Google and guests both love.

  3. Room and Amenity Pages

    Each room type deserves its own dedicated page — not just a section on the homepage. A page titled "Dar Suite with Private Terrace — Riad Marrakech" can rank for specific, high-converting search terms that a generic homepage never will. Include 300+ words per room page, professional photos with keyword-tagged alt text, and a direct booking widget.

  4. Image Optimization

    Every image needs a descriptive filename (e.g., riad-marrakech-rooftop-terrace-sunset.jpg, not IMG_3847.jpg) and an alt text that describes the scene while naturally including your target keyword. Compress all images to under 150KB using WebP format — page speed directly impacts Google ranking, and Marrakech riad sites are notoriously image-heavy and slow.

  5. Schema Markup (Structured Data)

    Implement Hotel schema and LocalBusiness schema on your site. This tells Google your riad's name, address, phone number, check-in times, amenities, price range, and aggregate rating — making you eligible for rich snippets in search results that dramatically increase click-through rates vs plain blue links.

  6. Internal Linking

    Build a logical internal link structure: the homepage links to room pages, room pages link to the booking page, blog articles link to room pages and the homepage. This distributes PageRank throughout your site and tells Google which pages are most important. Every blog post should contain 2–4 contextual links back to core service pages.

Local SEO

Dominating Local Search
in Marrakech's Medina

Local SEO determines whether your riad appears in Google Maps results and the "Local Pack" — the 3-result map block that appears above organic results for searches like "riad Marrakech" or "boutique hotel Medina Marrakech." This placement is often more valuable than a standard organic #1 ranking.

Google Business Profile: Your Most Important Free Tool

Every riad in Marrakech must have a fully optimized Google Business Profile. This is non-negotiable and completely free. An optimized profile includes:

  • Accurate NAP data — Name, Address, Phone must be identical across your website, GBP, and all directories
  • Primary category: "Bed and Breakfast" or "Boutique Hotel" — with secondary categories like "Riad" if available
  • 40+ professional photos — interior, exterior, rooms, rooftop, courtyard, food. Profiles with more photos get significantly more views
  • Weekly Google Posts — short updates about seasonal offers, events, or property features keep your profile active
  • Q&A section populated — proactively add and answer common questions (check-in time, parking, pool access) to control the narrative
  • Review response rate of 100% — respond to every review, positive or negative, within 24 hours
Critical warning

If your riad has duplicate Google Business Profile listings — which is common in the Medina due to historical submissions — Google will split your review count and ranking signals between them. Identify and merge or remove duplicates immediately. Duplicates are one of the most common reasons riads underperform in local search.

NAP Consistency: The Foundation of Local Authority

Google cross-references your riad's Name, Address, and Phone number across hundreds of websites to verify you are who you say you are. If these details differ between your website, Booking.com listing, TripAdvisor profile, Yelp, and your GBP — even slightly (Street vs Rue, different phone formats) — it erodes your local ranking authority.

Audit and standardize your NAP data across: Google Business Profile, TripAdvisor, Booking.com, Airbnb, Lonely Planet, Fodor's, Yelp, and any Moroccan tourism directories you appear in. Use the exact same format everywhere.

Generating Reviews That Rank

Reviews are both a ranking signal and a conversion tool. Riads with 100+ Google reviews and a 4.8+ average rating consistently outperform competitors in local pack results. Build a systematic review generation process:

  • Ask at checkout, in person, when the guest experience is freshest
  • Send a follow-up WhatsApp 48 hours after checkout with a direct link to your Google review page
  • Include a QR code in your room welcome card that links directly to your review form
  • Never incentivize reviews — Google's algorithm detects unusual review velocity and may penalize you
Technical SEO

Technical Foundations
That Google Demands

Technical SEO issues are invisible to guests but fully visible to Google. Many riad websites in Marrakech — built on WordPress templates or by local web designers without SEO training — have fundamental technical issues that prevent Google from crawling and indexing their content properly.

Core Web Vitals: Speed as a Ranking Factor

Since 2021, Google uses Core Web Vitals as a direct ranking factor. For riads, the most common failures are:

  • Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) above 4 seconds — caused by uncompressed images. Convert all images to WebP, lazy-load below-the-fold images, and use a CDN. Target LCP under 2.5s.
  • Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS) above 0.25 — caused by images without specified dimensions, fonts loading late, or embedded maps shifting content. Specify width and height on all images.
  • Interaction to Next Paint (INP) above 500ms — caused by heavy JavaScript. Audit and remove unused scripts from booking widgets and chat plugins.
Quick test

Run your riad website through PageSpeed Insights (pagespeed.web.dev) and GTmetrix right now. Most riad sites score below 50/100 on mobile — the version Google uses for ranking. A score below 50 is almost certainly costing you search positions.

Mobile-First Optimization

Over 65% of travel searches happen on mobile devices. Google indexes the mobile version of your site first — your desktop design is secondary. Your riad website must have a fully responsive layout, touch-friendly navigation, readable text without zooming, and a booking flow that works flawlessly on a phone screen.

HTTPS and Security

All riad websites must run on HTTPS (the padlock icon). HTTP sites are flagged as "Not Secure" in Chrome, kill booking conversion, and receive a ranking penalty from Google. If your site still uses HTTP, this is the first fix — your hosting provider can add an SSL certificate in minutes.

XML Sitemap and Robots.txt

Submit an XML sitemap to Google Search Console listing all your important pages — homepage, room pages, blog posts, and contact page. Ensure your robots.txt file is not accidentally blocking Googlebot from crawling your site — a common mistake after website migrations that can cause your entire site to disappear from search results.

Off-Page SEO

Building Backlinks That
Move the Needle

Backlinks — other websites linking to yours — remain one of Google's strongest ranking signals. For riads in Marrakech, link building requires a combination of press outreach, directory listings, and content partnerships. Not all links are equal: a link from The Guardian Travel or Condé Nast Traveler is worth 100x more than a link from a generic directory.

High-Value Link Sources for Riads

  • Travel media — Lonely Planet, The Guardian Travel, Telegraph Travel, Le Monde Voyager. Build these through press trip invitations and journalist outreach.
  • Travel bloggers — Partner with English, French, and German travel bloggers who cover Morocco and Marrakech. Offer a complimentary stay in exchange for a genuine, link-included review post.
  • Local tourism authority links — Ensure you're listed on the Office National Marocain du Tourisme website and any Marrakech city tourism pages.
  • Morocco travel guides — Many authoritative "Best Riads in Marrakech" list pages exist. Reach out to site owners to request inclusion. A placement on one well-ranked listicle can send sustained traffic for years.
  • Wedding and event directories — If your riad hosts events, listings on wedding directories provide niche, relevant backlinks.
Avoid these

Never buy backlinks from link farms or mass-submit to low-quality directories. Google's Penguin algorithm penalizes unnatural link profiles. A single manual penalty can remove your site from Google results entirely — a catastrophic outcome that can take 6–12 months to recover from.

The Content Flywheel: Earning Links Through Publishing

The most sustainable link-building strategy is publishing content so good that other sites want to link to it. A definitive guide like "The Complete Neighborhood Guide to Marrakech's Medina" or "Historic Riads of the Medina: Architecture and Heritage" naturally attracts links from travel sites, journalists, and bloggers who find it useful. This is called link-earning, not link-building — and it compounds over time.

Multilingual SEO

English, French, and Arabic —
Reaching Every Market

Marrakech's inbound tourism market is genuinely multilingual, and a monolingual website leaves significant revenue on the table. Here is how to structure multilingual SEO correctly:

The Three Languages Your Riad Needs

French (FR) is your #1 priority. France is consistently Morocco's largest source market for inbound tourists. A French-language version of your site isn't a nice-to-have — it's the difference between reaching 30% or 70%+ of your addressable European market. Build full French-language pages with French-language content, not just a Google Translate toggle.

English (EN) targets UK, American, Canadian, Australian, and Northern European markets. The growing British and American interest in Marrakech makes this your second most important language.

Arabic (AR) is strategically important for Gulf traveler segments (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait), who tend to book higher-value accommodations and longer stays. Arabic-language SEO remains underexploited by most Marrakech riads — a significant opportunity.

Implementing Hreflang Correctly

The hreflang HTML attribute tells Google which language version of a page to show to users in different countries. Incorrect implementation is one of the most common multilingual SEO mistakes — it can cause Google to show French users your English page, or worse, treat your language versions as duplicate content. Each language version must have reciprocal hreflang tags referencing all other versions, including a self-referencing tag.

URL structure for multilingual riads

Use subdirectories for language versions: yourriad.com/en/, yourriad.com/fr/, yourriad.com/ar/. This concentrates domain authority on a single root domain — more effective than separate domains or subdomains for most riad sites.

Content That Ranks and
Converts Browsers to Bookers

Content marketing for riads serves two simultaneous goals: building topical authority with Google and building emotional connection with potential guests. Every blog post you publish is a permanent asset that can drive traffic and bookings years from now.

Content Types That Work for Riads

  • Neighborhood guides — "The Complete Guide to Staying in the Marrakech Medina", "Guéliz vs Medina: Where to Stay in Marrakech". These rank for informational searches and build authority.
  • Experience posts — "A Morning in the Souks from our Riad", "Sunset from a Marrakech Rooftop Terrace". First-person narrative content that creates an emotional pull to book.
  • Practical guides — "How to Get from Marrakech Airport to the Medina", "Best Restaurants Near Jemaa el-Fnaa", "Marrakech in Ramadan: What to Expect". These attract guests in planning mode and associate your riad with helpful expertise.
  • Seasonal content — "Marrakech in December: Why Winter is the Best Time to Visit a Riad". Target low-season searches and educate guests on year-round appeal.

Publishing Cadence

Consistency matters more than volume. A riad publishing two quality posts per month will outperform one publishing ten thin posts and then going silent. Build a 12-month editorial calendar based on keyword research and guest questions you receive regularly — then execute consistently.
