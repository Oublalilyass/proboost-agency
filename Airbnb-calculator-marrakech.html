Common Questions

Airbnb Revenue FAQ

Everything you need to know about Airbnb income in Marrakech.

How much can I earn on Airbnb in Marrakech?
+
The average Airbnb revenue in Marrakech varies significantly by property type and location. A small 2-room apartment in Gueliz might earn 15,000–25,000 MAD/month, while a well-positioned 6-room riad in the medina typically earns 60,000–120,000 MAD/month. Luxury riads with pools in Hivernage can exceed 300,000 MAD/month during peak season (October–April). Use our calculator above to estimate your specific property's potential.
What is the average occupancy rate for Airbnb Marrakech?
+
Marrakech has a clear seasonal pattern. Peak season (October–April) sees average occupancy rates of 70–85% for well-listed properties. Shoulder season (May, September) averages 55–65%. Summer (June–August) drops to 35–50% due to extreme heat, though European visitors still book during school holidays. Year-round average for a well-managed, well-reviewed listing is typically 60–70%.
What nightly rate should I charge for my Marrakech riad?
+
Nightly rates in Marrakech depend on property type, location, and amenities. Budget apartments in Gueliz: 250–400 MAD/night. Standard medina riads: 400–800 MAD/night per room. Boutique riads with pool: 700–1,500 MAD/night. Luxury riads and villas: 1,500–5,000+ MAD/night. These rates are per room per night for entire property listings — whole-property rates are typically 3–8× higher depending on room count.
How accurate is this Airbnb revenue calculator?
+
This calculator provides estimates based on your inputs and reflects general market averages for Marrakech in 2026. Actual revenue will vary based on your listing quality, photos, reviews, response rate, cancellation policy, and how well your property is marketed. The calculator does not account for expenses (utilities, staff, maintenance, taxes) or seasonal fluctuations — but it gives you a solid baseline for planning and pricing decisions.
How can I increase my Airbnb revenue in Marrakech?
+
The most effective strategies are: (1) Professional photography — listings with high-quality photos earn 40% more. (2) Optimized Airbnb SEO — the right title, description, and keywords determine your search ranking. (3) Review velocity — more positive reviews boost your ranking and conversion rate. (4) Direct booking website — reduces Airbnb fees by 15–20% on shifted bookings. (5) Dynamic pricing — tools like Wheelhouse or DPGO adjust rates by season and demand. ProBoost Agency specializes in all of these for Moroccan hosts.
Do I need a license to run Airbnb in Marrakech?
+
Morocco has specific regulations for short-term rentals. Properties must be registered with the Ministry of Tourism and obtain a classification (1–5 stars for riads). Hosts must register guests with local authorities (fiches d'hébergement). Income from Airbnb rentals is taxable in Morocco under the income tax code. We recommend consulting a Moroccan accountant or legal advisor for your specific situation. Compliance also helps your listing — Airbnb favors licensed, verified hosts in search rankings.
