Direct Booking in Marrakech: How Guests Pay Less and Owners Keep Every Dirham

Airbnb and Booking.com take up to 20% off every reservation. Here's how riad, villa and apartment owners in Marrakech are cutting out the middleman — and how travelers get a better price by booking direct.

01 Why Direct Booking Exists — And Why It Matters Now

Every time a guest books a riad, villa or apartment in Marrakech through Airbnb or Booking.com, the platform takes a cut before the owner sees a single dirham. That cut usually sits between 15% and 20% of the total reservation, split between host service fees and guest service fees baked into the price the traveler sees.

Direct booking simply means the guest reserves straight from the property — through its own website, a WhatsApp conversation, or a phone call — instead of going through a third-party marketplace. No middleman, no commission, no algorithm deciding who gets seen first.

For Marrakech's riads and independent hosts, this isn't a fringe idea anymore. With rising OTA fees and increasingly aggressive algorithm changes, more property owners are building their own booking presence alongside their Airbnb listing rather than relying on it exclusively.

The commission an OTA charges doesn't disappear — it's simply added to what the guest pays, or subtracted from what the owner keeps. Direct booking removes that layer entirely.

02 What OTA Commissions Really Cost a Marrakech Owner

Take a riad room that rents for the equivalent of $100 a night through Airbnb. The guest often pays a service fee on top, pushing the real price closer to $112–$115. Meanwhile the host, after Airbnb's own host-side fee, might only receive $85–$90 of that original $100.

Multiply that gap across a full year of bookings and the numbers become significant, especially for smaller riads with 4 to 8 rooms competing against larger guesthouses with dedicated revenue management teams.

The guest pays less, the owner keeps more — and that gap of roughly $25 to $30 per night is exactly what gets redistributed when a reservation moves off the marketplace.

03 Why Direct Bookings Are Cheaper for Travelers Too

This is the part guests rarely think about: booking direct isn't just good for the owner, it's usually better for the traveler's wallet as well. Because the property isn't paying a 15–20% commission, most riad and villa owners are happy to pass part of that saving on to guests who book directly — either as a flat discount, a free airport pickup, a room upgrade, or simply a lower listed rate than what appears on Airbnb.

  • No guest service fee. The extra $10–$20 Airbnb adds at checkout simply doesn't exist on a direct booking.
  • Negotiable extras. Owners can offer perks — breakfast, transfers, late checkout — that OTA pricing rules often restrict.
  • Direct communication. Guests deal straight with the riad, which usually means faster, more flexible answers about dates, group sizes, or special requests.
  • Better last-minute rates. Owners can quote a discounted price on the spot for direct enquiries without waiting on platform pricing tools.

The trade-off is that direct booking requires a bit more trust — you're not protected by Airbnb's dispute resolution system. That's why it matters to book direct with verified, reputable properties rather than anonymous listings found through a random search.

When you do reach out directly, a few habits keep the process safe:

  • Check the riad's reviews on Google and TripAdvisor, not just Airbnb, to confirm it's a real, established business.
  • Ask for recent photos or a short video call before sending any deposit.
  • Pay deposits through a traceable method — bank transfer to a registered business account, or a secure booking-engine link — rather than informal cash apps.
  • Get your dates, room type and total price confirmed in writing over email or WhatsApp.

06 How Owners Set Up Direct Booking, Step by Step

Riad and Airbnb owners don't need to abandon OTAs entirely — most successful direct-booking strategies run alongside Airbnb, gradually shifting repeat guests and new organic traffic toward a commission-free channel.

01

Build a fast, mobile-first booking page

A simple page with real photos, clear pricing and an availability calendar or WhatsApp enquiry button — most travelers book from their phone.

02

Get found on Google, not just Airbnb

Local SEO and a Google Business Profile matter more than people expect — many "book direct" searches start with a simple Google Maps search.

03

Reward direct bookings, don't just ask for them

A small discount, a free transfer or a welcome mint tea for guests who book direct gives them a real reason to skip the OTA next time.

04

Turn past Airbnb guests into repeat direct bookings

A simple follow-up message after checkout, inviting guests to book their next Marrakech trip directly, converts loyal guests without violating platform rules.

05

Track the split between OTA and direct revenue

Knowing exactly how much of your revenue still comes from commissioned channels makes it easy to measure progress month over month.

07 Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to book a riad directly instead of through Airbnb?

Yes, as long as you book through the property's official website or a verified WhatsApp/phone number. Check reviews on Google and TripAdvisor first, ask for photos or a video call, and pay through a traceable method such as a bank transfer to a registered business account or a secure booking-engine link.

How much can I save by booking direct in Marrakech?

Travelers typically save 10–20% compared to the same room on Airbnb, since owners can pass on part of the commission they no longer pay. Use the savings calculator above to estimate a specific property's numbers.

How do riad and Airbnb owners set up direct bookings?

Owners need a fast, mobile-friendly booking page or WhatsApp enquiry flow, clear pricing, strong photography and visibility on Google. Most start by adding a booking page and gradually directing repeat guests toward it for their next stay.

Does going direct mean leaving Airbnb altogether?

No — most owners keep their Airbnb listing for discovery and new guests, while building a direct channel for repeat guests and organic website traffic. Over time, the share of commission-free bookings simply grows.
