Expert Branding Advice

What Makes a Great
Business Name?

Before you register, run your favourite names through these proven criteria used by professional brand strategists.

⚡
Short & Memorable
The best brand names are 1–3 syllables and under 10 characters. Think Google, Apple, Nike, Stripe. Shorter names are easier to type, say, and remember. If someone can't spell it after hearing it once, reconsider.
🔊
Say It Out Loud
Say the name 10 times quickly. Does it flow naturally? Is it easy to pronounce across different accents? The "radio test": if a radio listener can understand and remember it without seeing it written, it's a strong name.
🌐
Domain Available
Check that the .com domain is available before committing. A matching .com is a significant trust signal — customers expect it. If the .com is taken, consider adding a prefix or suffix, or use .co, .io, or country-specific TLDs.
™
Trademark Check
Run your top choices through trademark databases in your country (USPTO for USA, EUIPO for Europe, OMPIC for Morocco). A name that's already trademarked in your industry can lead to costly legal problems later.
🌍
Cross-Cultural Check
If you plan to operate internationally, check that your name doesn't mean something negative in other languages. There are famous examples of brand names with unfortunate translations — this quick check can save major embarrassment.
🔮
Future-Proof It
Avoid names that lock you into a single product or geography. Amazon started as a bookstore — imagine if it had been named "Books.com." Leave room for your business to evolve. Abstract or coined names age better than overly literal ones.

Why Your Business Name Matters More Than You Think

Your business name is the first thing every customer, investor, and partner encounters. It shapes perception before anyone has seen your product, visited your website, or spoken to your team. A great business name is a strategic asset — it makes marketing easier, builds brand equity faster, and creates a foundation that compounds in value over years.

Research consistently shows that companies with short, distinctive, easy-to-pronounce names outperform their competitors on brand recall metrics by 2–3×. In a digital world where attention is scarce, the right name can be the difference between being remembered and being forgotten after 30 seconds.

"A name is a story compressed into a single word. The best business names make people curious, tell them something true, and stick in memory long after the first encounter."

Types of Business Names

Understanding the major categories of business names helps you choose the right strategy for your brand:

  • Descriptive names (Digital Ocean, General Electric) — tell you exactly what the business does. Easy to understand, harder to trademark.
  • Invented / coined words (Kodak, Xerox, Google) — completely original, highly trademarkable, but require marketing investment to build meaning.
  • Compound words (Facebook, Snapchat, PayPal) — combine two familiar words to create something new. Memorable and intuitive.
  • Metaphors (Amazon, Apple, Sprint) — borrow meaning from something unrelated. Create emotional associations and are highly memorable.
  • Founder names (Ford, Ferrari, Chanel) — build personal brand equity. Work best when the founder's reputation is the product's primary differentiator.
  • Acronyms (IBM, BMW, KPMG) — can work once established, but are hard to build from scratch as they carry no inherent meaning for new brands.

Business Naming for Morocco & North Africa

If you're naming a business in Morocco or targeting North African and Middle Eastern markets, there are specific considerations that apply:

  • Bilingual compatibility: Choose a name that works in both French and Arabic — the two dominant business languages in Morocco. Names with Arabic roots can build immediate cultural connection with local customers.
  • Cultural sensitivity: Avoid names that reference religion, royalty, or national symbols — these are protected and can create legal issues in Morocco.
  • Tourism and hospitality: For riads, hotels, and tour operators in Marrakech, names with references to geography (Atlas, Sahara, Medina), craftsmanship (Zellige, Arabesque), or nature (Palmeraie, Jardin) resonate strongly with international tourists.
  • Domain and social media: Check availability on Instagram and TikTok as well as .ma and .com domains. The .ma ccTLD builds strong local trust signals for Moroccan businesses.

After You've Chosen Your Name

Once you've landed on a business name you love, here's the essential checklist before you launch:

  • Check domain availability (.com and country TLD)
  • Check social media handle availability (@yourname on Instagram, Twitter/X, LinkedIn)
  • Run a trademark search in your operating country
  • Google the exact name — check for existing businesses, controversies, or confusing associations
  • Test pronunciation with 5+ people from different backgrounds
  • Check for any negative meaning in other languages (especially Arabic, French, Spanish)
  • Register the domain and social handles immediately — even before you officially launch
  • Brief a designer on your name's personality for the logo direction
